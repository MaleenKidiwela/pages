About

I study the Earth's subtle vibrations to reveal what lies beneath.

I'm a seismologist at the University of Washington specializing in ambient noise seismology and ocean-bottom geophysics. My work extracts meaningful signals from the continuous hum of the planet — what it tells us when no earthquakes are shaking it.

My current research spans Orca Volcano tomography and earthquake location, ambient noise cross-correlation of the Cascadia subduction zone, and monitoring submarine volcanism through the Ocean Observatories Initiative. Previously, I investigated methane seepage along the US North Atlantic with NOAA Ocean Explorer and developed mobile weather systems with drone and sensor technology.
